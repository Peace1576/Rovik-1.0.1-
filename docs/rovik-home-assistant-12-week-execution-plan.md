# Rovik Home Assistant 12-Week Execution Plan

## Goal

Ship an alpha-quality Eve focused on home admin, digital life management, and household money operations for 10 to 20 test households.

## Outcomes by week 12

- Eve is clearly positioned as a home operations assistant
- six core workflows are visible in product and demo
- trust model is live
- alpha users can complete real household tasks weekly
- founder has usage evidence for fundraising and accelerator applications

## Week 1

Product:

- lock the product wedge to home operations
- finalize six MVP workflows

Engineering:

- clean positioning copy across landing, README, and settings
- define shared types for household objects

Growth:

- write alpha user profile and outreach list

## Week 2

Product:

- define permission ladder and action history requirements

Engineering:

- add household entities schema: bills, subscriptions, vendors, routines, devices, projects
- instrument core usage events

Growth:

- draft alpha onboarding flow

## Week 3

Product:

- design Home Brief experience

Engineering:

- build Home Brief aggregation logic from weather, calendar, tasks, and inbox signals
- add source-linked sections to outputs

Growth:

- create demo script for investors and alpha users

## Week 4

Product:

- design Inbox workflow with action extraction

Engineering:

- ship household inbox triage states: action, reminder, archive, draft
- add task creation from email

Growth:

- recruit first 5 alpha households

## Week 5

Product:

- define Bills and Subscriptions dashboard

Engineering:

- detect receipts, renewals, due notices, and recurring charges from inbox data
- add due-soon and review-needed views

Growth:

- build household pain-point survey

## Week 6

Product:

- define Savings and Purchase Assistant workflow

Engineering:

- add comparison flow for home purchases
- add savings recommendations based on subscriptions and price changes

Growth:

- collect first usage interviews

## Week 7

Product:

- design Home Task Operator experience

Engineering:

- add recurring routines, reminders, lists, and maintenance tasks
- connect reminders and notifications cleanly

Growth:

- package three use-case demos: parents, busy professional, smart-home user

## Week 8

Product:

- define Smart Home control guardrails

Engineering:

- finalize Home Assistant routine execution with approvals
- add away, bedtime, and energy-saving flows

Growth:

- recruit 5 to 10 more households

## Week 9

Product:

- refine voice-first interaction around household workflows

Engineering:

- improve latency, interruptions, and transcript polish
- make proactive suggestions visible but non-intrusive

Growth:

- create landing page screenshots and short product video

## Week 10

Product:

- review retention blockers from alpha use

Engineering:

- fix workflow friction points
- harden edge cases, failed integrations, and permission flows

Growth:

- prepare investor narrative using alpha learnings

## Week 11

Product:

- package the alpha as a coherent household system

Engineering:

- finalize dashboards, trust states, and action history
- add weekly summary and due-soon alerts

Growth:

- collect testimonials and quantified wins

## Week 12

Product:

- decide go-forward scope for beta

Engineering:

- stabilize the product for broader testing
- document architecture and operational readiness

Growth:

- publish waitlist, founder story, and alpha case studies

## Operating cadence

Every week:

- Monday: priorities and workflow decisions
- Wednesday: build review and bug triage
- Friday: alpha feedback review and metric readout

Every two weeks:

- workflow usability interviews
- roadmap cut or continue decisions

## Metrics to review weekly

- WAH: weekly active households
- briefs opened
- inbox actions completed
- bills flagged before due date
- subscription reviews completed
- tasks created and completed
- device routines triggered
- user-reported trust score

## Team priorities

Founder:

- product decisions
- alpha recruitment
- fundraising narrative

Engineering:

- workflow reliability
- latency
- action transparency

Operations:

- alpha onboarding
- support loop
- weekly metrics and customer synthesis

## Kill criteria

Pause expansion if:

- week-4 retention is weak
- users do not trust execution flows
- product usage clusters around only one workflow and the rest are dead weight

## Success condition

By the end of 12 weeks, users should describe Eve as:

"the assistant that keeps my home life under control."
